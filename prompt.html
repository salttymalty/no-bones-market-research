## THE SHOW

**Title:** No Bones About It
**Creators:** Dave Hudson (book & lyrics) and Paul Libman (music)
**Credentials:** Two-time Richard Rodgers Award winners. Dave has written 21+ musicals and teaches playwriting and lyric writing in Chicago. Paul built a successful commercial music company and was fronting his own jazz trio at 17.
**Website:** https://libmanhudson.com/shows/no-bones-about-it/
**Licensing:** Northern Sky Licensing — (920) 854-6117 — https://northernskylicensing.com/

**Concept:** Romeo and Juliet hilariously reimagined in the world of competitive barbecue. At the Verona International Ribfest, two rival smokehouses — Monty's Jurassic Pork (perfects secret sauces) and Cappy's Happy Pig (scientifically determines the dry rub) — are locked in a feud. Their children Ronny and Julie are "char-crossed" lovers who desperately try to hide their romance while their families compete for the top prize. The situation unravels when food bloggers with smartphones arrive. Happy ending (unlike the original).

**Tagline:** "Two smokehouses, both alike in dignity"

**Cast:** 3 women, 4 men (7 total — very small, very licensable)
**Band:** Piano, bass, drums, guitar — acoustic and electric (4 piece)
**Songs include:** "We Love Food," "Variety is the Spice of Life," "No Bones About It"

**Production History:**
- World premiere at Northern Sky Theater, Peninsula State Park, Door County, Wisconsin (2015)
- Return engagement at Northern Sky (2016)
- Revival at Northern Sky, 2025 summer season (current)

**Critical Reception:** Described as "funny, tuneful, and has that wonderful momentum you crave in a musical."

**Why This Show Has a Built-In Marketing Advantage:** The show IS about BBQ culture. Audiences in BBQ regions will recognize and delight in the specificity. Cross-promotion with local smokehouses, BBQ festivals, and competition circuits is natural. No other musical has this advantage.

## BBQ REGIONS CONTEXT

- **Eastern NC:** Whole hog, vinegar-and-pepper sauce. Purist. (Raleigh, Goldsboro, Wilmington)
- **Western NC / Lexington style:** Pork shoulder, vinegar-tomato sauce. (Asheville, Lexington, Charlotte)
- **South Carolina:** Mustard-based "Carolina Gold" — the state's signature. (Greenville, Columbia, Charleston)
- **Memphis, TN:** Dry rub ribs and wet ribs. The competition capital.
- **Nashville, TN:** Hot chicken crossover, dry rub traditions.
- **Georgia:** Sweet tomato-based sauce. (Atlanta, Savannah)
- **Virginia:** Sweet and tangy. (Richmond)

The show's sauce-vs-rub rivalry maps directly to real regional debates.

## MARKETING TACTICS

### Theatre Acquisition
- "Pairs With Your City" one-sheets per market
- Staged reading tour (3-5 theatres, low commitment)
- "East Coast Premiere" exclusivity pitch
- Season timing to BBQ competition season (May-October)

### Cross-Promotion
- Dinner-and-show packages with BBQ joints
- BBQ competition booth or musical number
- Tourism board partnerships (free co-marketing)
- Sauce/rub brand lobby sponsorship
- Food truck outside theatre on show nights

### Audience Engagement
- Lobby BBQ tastings
- "Montague or Capp?" wristband vote
- Opening night parking lot cookoff
- Merch: aprons, sauce bottles, "Char-Crossed Lovers" tees

### Digital
- Song video from 2025 revival
- "BBQ or Shakespeare?" social quiz
- #CharCrossedLovers photo campaign
- Behind-the-scenes rehearsal content
