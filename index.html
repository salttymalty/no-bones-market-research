Marketing Tactics

Five categories of approach, from theatre acquisition to digital content.

Theatre Acquisition

  • "Pairs With Your City" custom one-sheets showing local BBQ density
  • Staged reading tour at 3–5 target theatres (low commitment entry)
  • "East Coast Premiere" or "Carolinas Premiere" exclusivity pitch
  • Season timing aligned to BBQ competition season (May–October)

Cross-Promotion

  • "Dinner and a show" packages with local BBQ joints
  • Booth or musical number at BBQ competitions
  • BBQ trail tourism board partnerships (free co-marketing)
  • Sauce/rub brand sponsorship for lobby presence
  • BBQ food truck parked outside theatre on show nights

Media & Press

  • "Shakespeare meets smoker" — inherently press-friendly concept
  • BBQ competition YouTube channels and social media communities
  • Local food bloggers/influencers to opening night + lobby tasting
  • Richard Rodgers Award angle for arts press credibility

Audience Engagement

  • Pre-show BBQ tasting events in the lobby
  • "Are you a Montague or a Capp?" wristband vote
  • Opening night BBQ cookoff in the parking lot
  • Aprons, sauce bottles, "Char-Crossed Lovers" merch

Digital & Content

  • Short video of key songs from the 2025 Northern Sky revival
  • "BBQ or Shakespeare?" social media quiz
  • #CharCrossedLovers couples-at-BBQ-restaurants campaign
  • Behind-the-scenes revival rehearsal documentation

Questions for Dave

Information needed to turn research into action. Organized by category.
Category Question Why It Matters
Rights What's the current licensing status? Exclusively through Northern Sky Licensing, or can you license directly? Determines who theatres contact for rights
Rights What's the licensing fee structure? Per-performance, flat fee, negotiable for smaller theatres? Community vs. regional theatres have very different budgets
Rights Is there flexibility on cast/band size? Can it work with 5 actors or piano-only? Expands the pool of eligible theatres
Rights Any geographic restrictions or holdbacks? Don't pitch a theatre that can't license it
Materials Does a marketing kit exist? One-sheet, synopsis, character descriptions, press photos? If not, building one is step one
Materials Are there production photos from 2015/2016? High-res? Every pitch email needs a visual
Materials Is there any audio or video recording? Even a rehearsal capture? The single most persuasive asset for an artistic director
Materials Press reviews or pull quotes from Northern Sky runs? Social proof for the pitch
Materials How does an interested theatre get a perusal copy of the script? The funnel from "interested" to "committed" must be frictionless
Strategy Has the show been pitched to theatres outside Northern Sky before? What happened? Don't retrace failed paths
Strategy Do you have relationships with any East Coast theatres? Warm intros beat cold emails 10:1
Strategy Are you open to attending staged readings or opening nights? Playwright presence at a reading is a huge draw
Strategy Do you want to lead this effort, or do you want a partner driving it? Defines the working relationship
Production What worked at Northern Sky that was specific to Door County vs. universal? Helps tailor the pitch per market
Production Which songs land hardest with audiences? Lead with the strongest material in pitches
Production Any production challenges? Set, technical, casting? Theatres want to know it's producible
Production What's the target audience age range in practice? "Family-friendly BBQ comedy" vs. "adult comedy" are different pitches

Recommended Sequence

From here to first pitch.
  1. Get answers from Dave

    Rights, materials, appetite, and relationships — this shapes everything that follows.

  2. Build the marketing kit

    One-sheet, press photos, pull quotes, and a clear perusal process. If recordings exist from the 2025 revival, capture them.

  3. Tier the target list

    Top 10 get personal pitches, next 20 get a polished email, the rest get a listing. Start with Barn Dinner Theatre and Raleigh Little Theatre.

  4. Time it to the 2025 revival

    "Currently in revival at Northern Sky" is the strongest possible lead sentence. Fresh reviews, fresh photos, and momentum.

  5. Start with 2–3 warm targets

    Prioritize dinner theatres in the Carolinas and any theatre where Dave has a personal connection.

  6. Build festival presence

    Memphis in May (May 13–16) and the DC BBQ Battle (June 27–28) are concrete dates to plan around.
